## **Ex. No. 10 – Business Reports & Case Studies using Prompt Patterns**

### **Aim:**

To design, implement, and evaluate prompt-engineering techniques for generating high-quality business reports and case studies using Large Language Models (LLMs). This experiment focuses on demonstrating how structured prompting strategies enhance clarity, logical organization, factual reliability, and professional tone in business documentation compared to unstructured prompts.

---

### **AI Tools Required:**

ChatGPT or any advanced Large Language Model (LLM) such as Google Gemini or Microsoft Copilot for generating and refining business-oriented textual content .

---

### **Algorithm:**

1. Identify different business communication formats such as reports, case studies, proposals, and strategic briefs based on real-world scenarios.
2. Study and understand advanced prompt patterns including query decomposition, decision-making prompts, semantic filtering, answer engineering, and tail generation techniques.
3. Select a relevant business domain or scenario such as AI in marketing, predictive analytics in retail, or digital transformation strategies.
4. Generate initial outputs using simple, unstructured prompts to observe baseline performance.
5. Refine prompts by incorporating structural constraints, tone control, and logical segmentation using advanced prompt patterns.
6. Compare outputs from basic and refined prompts in terms of clarity, coherence, completeness, and professionalism.
7. Apply fact-checking and validation techniques to ensure accuracy and consistency of information.
8. Produce a final polished business document that meets industry-level standards of presentation and content quality .

### **Procedure:**

#### **Understanding Prompt Patterns:**

* **Query Decomposition:** Breaking down complex business problems into smaller, manageable components for precise output generation.
* **Decision Making:** Directing the AI to evaluate and choose between multiple strategies or solutions based on given criteria.
* **Answer Engineering:** Structuring the response into predefined sections such as introduction, analysis, findings, and conclusion.
* **Semantic Filtering:** Ensuring that the generated content maintains a formal tone, domain relevance, and professional language.
* **Tail Generation:** Extending the output with actionable insights, recommendations, or future implications.
* **Fact-Check List:** Reviewing generated content for correctness, especially numerical data, claims, and logical consistency .
